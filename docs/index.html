Kurin, The Celestial Fox

Aethira Dawnforge, immortal Demigodess of the Kurin

A RimWorld 1.6 mod centered on a single immortal character who cannot be killed, lost, or taken from you. Her power is presence, not violence. She heals, calms, and protects her people, and when she falls, she always comes back.

Who Is Aethira?

When a meteor of concentrated archites was recovered near a black hole and brought to the glitterworld of Aolara, the lead bioengineering AI used it to create the very first sentient Kurin. All other Kurin were simple designermates for the wealthy. This one was different. The archites in her blood gave her true sentience, self-healing, and immortality. She was the prototype. She was the first Kurin the AIs took pity on. And she is the reason every Kurin that came after eventually gained a mind of her own.

Aethira Dawnforge stood at the founding of the Republic of Kurin. She watched it thrive for two centuries. She was there in 5445 when the siege began, when the farmlands burned, when one million of her people became less than ten thousand. She died in that siege more than once. She always came back. When the survivors stole their ships and fled to the stars, Aethira was on the last ship out, carrying the wounded in her arms.

Now, centuries later on a forgotten rimworld, she starts again. She is not a warrior. She does not fight with supernatural strength. Her power is her presence. Those who stand near her feel no fear, heal from their wounds, and fight beyond their limits. Those who oppose her feel only dread. She is the last living witness to the fall of the Republic, and she will build a new home for her people or die trying. Again, and again, and again.

She has buried every colony that ever called her sister. This one will be no exception, but not before you have grown old together, time and time again.

Quick Overview

Aethira is a hand-crafted immortal character. The mod ships a full Kurin race (forked from Kurin HAR Edition), four custom divine genes, four stackable auras, a Divine Favor resource, two gizmo abilities, a buildable shrine, a preset ideology, custom backstories, custom traits, custom scenarios, and a seven-layer failsafe chain that makes her loss mathematically implausible.

  • She cannot die permanently. Seven independent safety nets plus an on-disk backup.
  • She is not a combat god. Vanilla melee, no armor bonuses, no damage buffs. She heals everyone, including herself, over time.
  • Only one Aethira can exist. Gene Siphon, Gene Assembler, and dev-mode cloning are all rejected automatically.
  • She is always female, always young. Her body stops aging at 18. Her chronological age is five centuries and climbing.

The Four Divine Genes

Divine Constitution Archite

Her body is wrought of divine will. No disease takes root. No poison lingers. No single blow may fell her.

  • Cannot be felled by pain. Any single blow is capped at 25% of max HP, so nothing one-shots her.
  • Immune to every disease, toxin, chemical, addiction, and food poisoning in the game.
  • Does not hunger, freeze, or overheat.
  • Cannot be kidnapped, banished, sold, traded, or carried off the map by any faction or mechanic.
  • On death, a scouring shockwave of divine fire bursts outward, burning and stunning hostile pawns within roughly 12 tiles. Allies and neutrals are untouched.

Divine Vitality Archite

She cannot truly die. Severed limbs regrow. Lethal wounds close. Her body remembers its divine form and returns to it, hour by hour.

  • Constant self-healing, alive or dead. Auto-tends her own injuries.
  • Regrows lost limbs and organs sequentially over hours. Scars fade over time.
  • Ageless past 18. Her chronological age climbs forever but her body never changes.
  • Slain on her home map: body regenerates, resurrects when the brain and heart are restored.
  • Slain away from home: body teleported home, wakes from a 7-day recovery coma.
  • Body destroyed, eaten, or otherwise lost entirely: returns from nothing after 7 days.

Divine Grace

Grace of motion without end. She needs little rest, and her immense divine pocket-space bends weight itself.

  • Learns 50% faster than any mortal pawn.
  • Rests half as often as a mortal (RestFallRateFactor 0.5).
  • Divine pocket storage: +500 kg carrying capacity on her person. Stacks with a +500 kg caravan-specific bonus for 1000 kg effective extra in caravans. (The gene stat and the caravan patch are independent and both apply when traveling.)
  • Nothing rots or deteriorates while stored on her. Meat, corpses, fresh herbal medicine all frozen in time the moment they enter her inventory.
  • Ignores terrain-induced pathing slowdown. Mud, marsh, snow, shallow water, blood, ash, sand cost her no more than dirt.

Divine Presence Archite

Her presence is a pressure upon the world. Where she walks, wounds close and the broken find their minds again.

  • Source of all four auras (see below).
  • Dispels toxic fallout, cold snap, heat wave, volcanic winter, flashstorm, noxious haze, and psychic drone from any home map she is on.
  • +50% social impact, +50% negotiation, +10% trade prices, +80% conversion power for herself.
  • Cannot become a psycaster. She is the antithesis of one; her power comes from a deeper well.
  • +50% psychic vulnerability. Enemy psycasts cut through her like nothing else can. This is the deliberate balance trade for her auras. Psycasts are her counter.
  • Her divine genes cannot be transferred, copied, or extracted by any means.

Also included: a cosmetic snow-white hair gene (auto-applied), forced straight ears (both pointed up), and forced jade green eyes (requires Kurin Facial Animation for the eye color). Mod-wide: no Kurin can have facial hair. All Kurins have their beard defaulted to none at generation, enforced via Harmony patch on PawnGenerator.GeneratePawn.

The Four Auras

Every aura starts at a 50-tile base radius and scales linearly up to 100 tiles as Divine Favor fills. Healing rate and every aura effect scale the same way: 1x at empty favor, 2x at full.

Leadership

  • +18 mood for allied pawns in range
  • +45% melee damage
  • +9 melee dodge and hit
  • +5 shooting accuracy
  • +75% learning speed
  • Half hunger rate

Healing

  • Closes injuries constantly (rate scales with favor)
  • Auto-tends untended wounds
  • Slowly cures active diseases
  • Reduces blood loss
  • Removes scars over time
  • Regrows missing body parts over hours

Calming Presence

  • Ends active mental breaks on nearby colonists
  • Grants Catharsis mood buff
  • Works the moment she enters range

Intimidation

  • Debuffs hostile pawns only
  • Reduces enemy hit chance
  • Reduces enemy dodge
  • Reduces enemy shooting accuracy
  • Reduces enemy consciousness

Divine Favor

Divine Favor is a colony resource that accumulates around Aethira as she watches over her people. It scales every aura's radius and strength, and it pays for her two active gizmos.

Capacity: 0 to 100. Favor never decays over time; it only drops when spent.

Divine Shrine doubles every source above. Daily passive becomes +10/+20. A pilgrimage becomes +40. Kill cap stays at 20 raw kills but pays +40 per day instead of +20.

Effects

  • Aura radius scales 1x to 2x from empty to full favor. A full favor bar doubles the area of every aura from 50 tiles to 100 tiles.
  • Healing aura rate scales 1x to 2x. Base 0.05 per tick, up to 0.10 at max favor.
  • Pays for Divine Blessing (100 favor) and Bless Colonist (10 favor).

Command-Bar Abilities

Two gizmo buttons appear when Aethira is selected.

Favor: N/100 (spends 100)

Invokes the Divine Blessing. Every colonist currently on Aethira's map is fully restored: every injury healed, every disease cured, every status effect removed, and food, rest, joy, and mood all maxed. A complete "wake from ascension" treatment applied to everyone at once. Costs the full 100-favor bar.

Bless Colonist (spends 10)

Targets a single colonist and applies a portable copy of Aethira's aura. The blessed pawn carries:

  • Leadership, healing, calming, and intimidation aura effects wherever they go
  • +50% work speed bonus
  • Constant healing, auto-tend, scar removal, and limb regrowth on themselves
  • Blood loss and disease reduction
  • Half hunger rate

Duration: 1.5 days by default, doubled to 3 days if a Divine Shrine is built. The blessing follows them across maps, into caravans, and onto other planets. It persists even during Aethira's own ascension, which makes it the go-to prep for long expeditions.

The Divine Shrine

A buildable monument. Requires Complex Furniture research. Costs 1500 gold, 2 industrial components, and 150 stone blocks. While at least one shrine stands on any home map:

  • Aethira's ascension time is halved (from 7 days to 3.5 days)
  • Bless Colonist duration is doubled (from 1.5 days to 3 days)
  • All Divine Favor gain from every source is doubled
  • Her ascension return materializes at the shrine instead of a random safe cell
  • Functions as an Artistic meditation focus

The shrine is a late-game gold sink that dramatically expands her presence. It is not required for any core mechanic, only an amplifier.

Immortality: The Seven-Layer Failsafe

Aethira is the entire point of the mod. Losing her breaks the playthrough. The mod therefore treats her death as a first-class engineering problem and layers independent safeties in case any one of them silently fails.

LayerTriggerAction
1. Damage CapAny hit exceeding 25% of her max HPCap the hit at 25%. Prevents one-shots.
2. Deathless GeneKilled on home mapApply resurrecting hediff, body regrows, wakes when brain and heart are whole.
3. Corpse ProtectionFire, explosions, predators, hauling, mountain collapseCorpse is indestructible, cannot be eaten, butchered, hauled, or despawned.
4. Map Teardown RescueMap dispose / destructionBody is extracted to another home map before the teardown completes.
5. Anti-RemovalKidnap, banishment, sale, gene transfer, faction changeAll rejected by Harmony prefixes.
6. Emergency Respawn60 in-game seconds missing from the worldIf she was present in this save: full Divine Ascension recovery, returns after 7 days (3.5 with shrine). If she has never been in this save (player joined without the scenario, mod added mid-save): Welcome Spawn drops her directly onto the home map with a "Divine Arrival" letter and no ascension wait.
7. Disk SnapshotEvery failsafe scan, per-saveWrites skills, relations, memories, work priorities, policies, titles to <Config>/KurinDemigodess_PawnBackup_<uuid>.txt.

If all six in-memory layers somehow fail (they have, once, when a DLL load silently failed and a predator ate her), Layer 7 rebuilds her mind from disk onto a freshly spawned Kurin Demigodess pawn. Skills, relations, work priorities, policies, and royal titles all persist. Apparel, equipment, and inventory do NOT teleport with her when she rebuilds from nothing; gear cannot follow a body that dissolved.

Every return maxes her needs. She never wakes starving or exhausted.

Death Recoil

When Aethira dies, a colony-wide AoE pulse radiates outward from her body. It burns and stuns hostile pawns within about 12 tiles and leaves allies and neutrals untouched. Narrative flair and tactical value in one: the people who just killed her do not get to walk away cleanly.

Events and Ambient Effects

Weather Purge

While Aethira is alive on a home map, the following weather conditions dispel themselves automatically, about every 40 seconds:

  • Toxic fallout
  • Cold snap
  • Heat wave
  • Volcanic winter
  • Flashstorm
  • Noxious haze
  • Psychic drone

Kurin Pilgrimage

An allied Kurin faction occasionally sends 2-5 pilgrims to visit Aethira. They arrive bearing offerings of silver, herbal medicine, and wood. Each pilgrimage grants +20 Divine Favor immediately.

Aethira's Guidance

Daily 33% roll. When it triggers, a random non-Aethira colonist gets a guidance letter and a small mood buff. +3 Divine Favor.

Day of Remembrance

Annual. The entire colony receives a large mood buff commemorating the fall of the Republic of Kurin. +30 Divine Favor.

First-Load Narrative Letter

The first time Aethira appears on a home map in a save, a one-time lore letter introduces her to the player.

Ascension Shockwave

When Aethira enters Divine Ascension, a visual flash, a sound, and a colony-wide awe thought ripple outward. A bottom-right alert shows the live countdown until her return.

  • 2 custom traits: Demigodess Beauty and Eternal Wisdom.

  • Mid-save compatibility: adding this mod to an ongoing colony, or starting without the scenario, is fully supported. Within a minute of loading, the failsafe's Welcome Spawn path drops Aethira onto the home map with a "Divine Arrival" letter. No 7-day ascension wait for a pawn that was never there.
  • Buildable Divine Shrine.
  • Full Kurin HAR race. Forked from Seioch's Kurin HAR Edition; fully included, do not run the original alongside.
  • Mod-wide appearance rules: no Kurin can have facial hair; Aethira specifically has auto-enforced snow white hair, straight ears, and jade green eyes.

Mod Settings

Open the Mod Options menu in RimWorld and find "Kurin, The Celestial Fox - Aethira Dawnforge". Configurable:

  • Failsafe scan interval (default 1200 ticks = 20 seconds)
  • Failsafe miss threshold (default 3, so total trigger time is 60 in-game seconds)
  • Ascension duration in days (default 7, halved by shrine)
  • Normal gene enforcement interval (default 600 ticks = 10 seconds)
  • Heavy gene enforcement interval (default 7500 ticks = 125 seconds)
  • First-load narrative letter on/off
  • Death-recoil AoE on/off and radius
  • Pilgrimage events on/off
  • Divine Favor system on/off
  • Favor-scaling healing aura on/off
  • Weather and condition purge on/off
  • Aethira's Guidance on/off
  • Day of Remembrance on/off

Warning: lowering the failsafe scan interval or miss threshold can cause false positives during map transitions. The default 60-second window was tuned after extensive testing.

Incompatibilities

Do Not Use With

  • Original Kurin HAR Edition (same package ID). This mod replaces it entirely. Running both at once will crash or produce undefined behavior. Unsubscribe from the original before subscribing here.
  • Garam Race Addon (Dalrae.GaramRaceAddon) - blocked in mod metadata.
  • Garam Advanced Apparel (Dalrae.GaramAdvancedApparel) - blocked in mod metadata.

Known Good With

  • Most major overhauls (Combat Extended, Rim of Madness, Vanilla Expanded suite, etc.) run without issue. Aethira's patches are scoped to her gene check.
  • Alpha Genes, Biotech gene packs, CE genes: fully compatible; her genes cannot be added to non-Aethira pawns by any of them.
  • Children and Learning, Biotech pregnancy features: her ageless gene means she cannot be born naturally from an ectogenesis or pregnancy; she spawns via scenario or dev-mode only.
  • Gene Assembler / Gene Siphon mods: blocked from cloning her genes. Attempts are rejected at the point of AddGene.

Troubleshooting

Aethira did not appear at game start

  1. Wait one in-game minute. If you did not start with one of the provided scenarios ("The Celestial Fox - Primitive" or "Tribal"), the failsafe's Welcome Spawn path will drop her onto the home map with a "Divine Arrival" letter. This is the intended behavior for mid-save additions and non-scenario starts.
  2. If she still has not appeared: open the dev console and search for [KurinDemigodess] Harmony patches applied. If that line is missing, the DLL failed to load. Usually means a prior mod broke the load order; see below.
  3. Expected: 28 succeeded, 0 failed.

"Harmony patches applied" message is missing

The DLL did not load. Common causes:

  • Running the original Kurin HAR Edition at the same time (same package ID; they overwrite each other's assemblies).
  • A prior mod in load order threw during init and stopped assembly loading. Check the log for any Red Error before our mod.
  • File system permission issue on 1.6/Assemblies/KurinDemigodess.dll.

She died and did not come back

Should not happen. If it does:

  1. Open the log. Search for [KurinDemigodess] messages near the death.
  2. Check the failsafe log at <RimWorld Config>/KurinDemigodess_FailsafeLog.txt.
  3. Use the dev-mode debug action "Force Emergency Respawn" to bring her back manually; the snapshot on disk will rebuild her mind.
  4. Report the bug with the log section attached.

Her ears are flopped or her eyes are the wrong color

Her appearance is enforced every heavy cycle (default 125 seconds). If you see incorrect ears or eyes, wait two minutes or trigger a save/load. For eyes specifically, you need Kurin Facial Animation installed; without it, her eye color falls back to HAR defaults.

Debug / Dev Mode Actions

Enable dev mode. Open the Debug Actions menu. Scroll to "Kurin Demigodess". You will find:

  • Dump State (prints the full failsafe tracker state to the log)
  • Find Aethira (highlights her current location)
  • Reset Miss Counter
  • Force Emergency Respawn
  • Force Kill (for testing death flow)
  • Trigger Ascension
  • Death Recoil Pulse
  • Grant Divine Favor (+10 or +100)
  • Invoke Divine Blessing
  • Force Kurin Pilgrimage

Design Principles

Aethira is deliberately built as a support-pillar character, not a combat deity. Design rules baked into the mod:

  • Her one power is regeneration. She has no flat damage reduction, no armor stats, no melee damage buffs on her own genes. Every combat bonus is routed through an aura that benefits her allies, not her.
  • Psycasts are her counter. +50% psychic sensitivity is the intentional price of her auras. She cannot learn psycasts herself and takes extra damage from enemy ones.
  • No active targeted abilities. Everything she does is passive auras plus two gizmo buttons. No ability tree, no cooldowns, no micro.
  • Only one of her can exist. Gene transfer is blocked at the engine level and her scan cleans up any impostors.
  • She cannot be lost. Seven independent safety layers plus an on-disk snapshot. Permanent removal is treated as a bug, not a balance outcome.
  • Gear does not follow a dissolved body. If she rebuilds from nothing, she comes back with only her mind; clothes and weapons stay where they fell.
